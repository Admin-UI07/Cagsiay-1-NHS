Abstract:


This study focuses on the impact of academic break to academic performance of selected Senior High School students at Cagsiay 1 National High School during the school year 2022-2023.


Specially, it sought to find out the answer to the following questions: what is the demographic profile of the respondents in terms of sex, age & grade level? What are the engaging activities do by the respondents during academic break? What is the impact of Academic performance. Social well-being, psychological impact, and Emotional behavior. Is there a significant relationship between academic break and activities engage during academic break? The study was conducted at Cagsiay 1 National High School, where the respondent in this study is grade 11 and 12 with the total 145 respondents.


Descriptive-Correlational Design is used in this study, in the analysis of the data gathered the following statistical treatment were used: Percentage distribution were used for profiling of the respondents. A weighted mean and Standard Deviation were applied to the Descriptive question part. The relationship if variables was treated using Pearson at 0.05 significant level And the Cochran's sample size is also used to get the sample size of the respondents.


The findings revealed that there is significant relationship between academic break and activities engage during the Academic Break.

Abstract:


The purpose of this study is to analyze the effects of modular distance learning and in-person classes on students’ social interaction, the perceive effects in social interaction encountered during modular distance learning, the perceive effects in social interaction of students during in-person classes, the perceive effects of modular distance learning in transition to in-person classes in terms behavior, social and culture.


The study expects to find no significant difference between modular distance learning and in-person classes on students’ social interaction thus will use T-test to determine the difference between the two (2) variables. The data were gathered using survey questionnaire which are distributed to the selected 88 grade 11 students from Cagsiay 1 National High School, thus the respondents were selected using the sub variant of probability sampling which is stratified random sampling. Descriptive T-test were used to analyze the accumulated data from 88 selected respondents.


The results revealed they there are significant difference between modular distance learning and in-person classes. In conclusion students interact more during in-person classes than modular distance learning wherein teachers should encourage the students to socially interact more during modular distance learning and they interact effectively during in-person classes.


Keywords: Modular, Distance, Learning, In-person, Classes

Abstract:


The study habit is one of the factor that would help the students to achieve their goals and to be successful. It is an everyday task like reading books, doing homework, listening in lectures, conducting group study and taking notes. Therefore, the purpose of this study is to determine the relationship of study habits and academic performance of the students.


Towards this study, it sought to answer the following questions: Demographic profile of the respondents in terms of age, sex, track/strand, Study habits of the respondents, Academic performance of the respondents. Factors affecting the study habits of the respondents in terms of: Knowledge and Interest, Peers, Family, Environment, Social Media, Teacher. Significant relationship between the factors affecting the study habits of the respondents and their academic performance. The total number of respondents were composed of one hundred fourthy one (141) selected senior high school students of Cagsiay 1 National high school in the school year 2022-2023. The researchers used a stratified random sampling method and researchers made checklist questionnaires which is 4point Likert scale. In addition, the researcher used descriptive comparative research methodology.


The results revealed that there is no significant relationship between the study habits and academic performance. Moreover, the researchers created an infographic to raise awareness to the students about the study habits. In addition, the study showed results that the respondents strongly agreed that their Study habits. However, the knowledge and interest are not factors that would affect their study habits on academic performance. Hence, most of the respondents agreed that their family, environment, social media, peers, and teachers is one of the factors that affects their study habits. In conclusion, the purpose of these study is to raise awareness to the students on how important study habits to their academic performance. As researchers, they would recommend to the students to be more focus and give their efforts to their study, because it is the one that should help them to get a good academic performance and to achieve their goals in life. Teachers may also use a different kind of strategies to focus and help their student to study hard and this can eventually encourage them to develop their study habits even in taking notes and studying for exams. Parents may give an extra time for their child study habits when it comes to academic performance even though they are busy, it is because their child spend most of the time at home with them. Nevertheless, they may provide a comfortable space to study. To the future researchers this study is recommended to them, this may serve as their guide and reference for those who willing to conduct a related study about the factors affecting the study habits on academic performance.


Keywords: Study Habits, Academic performance

While the researchers used a checklist form of questionnaire to gather the needed data for this study, Also, the researchers used the descriptive method of research in conducting this study.
